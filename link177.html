Fragment uit mijn fanfiction 'Gajeel's school of Magic',
gebaseerd op de anime/manga 'Fairy Tail'


Layton

 

There was an explosion. Floyd flew through the air and slammed into one of the pillars. It was a miracle there were any pillars left after so many years of us living in the school.

Lily and Rose were barely able to stand. When El Paco was busy attacking Floyd, they tried to gather their magic. They took that time to recover, instead of attacking the enemy while he was busy. They patiently waited till he finished. There was no strategy whatsoever.

I wasn’t able to land any attacks on El Paco. He seemed invincible. Besides the dust on his clothes and scratches he got from me blasting him into the back wall, he was unhurt. This was child’s play to him. It was just embarassing.

This was not how I envisioned my life as a mage would be. Not the way I would want it to end. In the first ever real lesson in magic, where we learned where our magic gathered, we saw a quick image of our future. Of what we were capable of doing.

I saw myself, reflected into water. Behind me there was a shadow. The water was the sea and my shadow covered an entire city. I was a giant. Just by extending my arm a little, I could take the moon into my hands. There were stars around me. I was Atlas, the Greek giant who carried the world on his shoulders. Literally.

I was nowhere near that size. I could never grow into that size. There wasn’t enough magic in this land.

But back on Lux, there was. I never told anyone what I did there.

One night, I snuck out from the place we were staying. Everyone was fast asleep. It had been a long day, training with the Fairy Tail gang. I shared a room with Kai, who was muttering in his sleep. It was the only time the demon particles still inside him could wake, but Ary and Jake had placed a very powerful seal. Well, about as powerful as they both could do at the time. So far, it hadn’t broken yet. So we didn't worry.

I jumped out the window and went to the beach. Ever since I first stepped on the land, I’d felt the magic pulsing through the ground, into my feet, my legs and spread throughout my body. I could hardly feel where the centre was. It was overwhelming and it stayed that way the entire time we were there.

To be honest, it drove me crazy. I had too much energy. I needed to blow off some steam.

So I went for a nighttime swim. There were few people around and no one questioned why I ran into the sea. The water was cool and at first blew the air out of my lungs, but only for a split second. I had only swum once before, in a pool. There wasn’t much technique, but I managed to go forward. The magic kind of helped.

I felt the water around me moving faster. At one point, I barely had to move at all. The magic was doing it for me. All I had to do, was come up to breathe.

After a few minutes, I slowed down. There was the sensation of sweating. I didn’t know how that was possible, there being water all around me and all.

I looked. There was no land in sight for miles. There was the moon, the light dancing in the water. There were stars, just like in my vision.

And it was so quiet. There was the sound of water touching my skin, my wet clothes. All of a sudden my breathing felt loud.

But still, it was not enough. There was too much magic still left. So I gathered it, as much as I could. It felt like trying to push two magnets together. It was wild, furious, nothing I’d ever felt before.

There wasn’t enough room. It either had to get out, or I needed to make more room. So I did. 

I started growing. My feet sank towards the ocean floor. Fish dashed away from my legs, startled by this intervention. My clothes shredded.

I grew so big the ocean seemed nothing more than a puddle around my feet. The moon was right in front of my face. The air around my shoulders was cold. If there was anyone awake they’d be terrified. I’m convinced even people from different time zones could see me.

If there were aliens, they probably could too.

As I stood there, my feet in the ocean and my head in space, there was peace. The magic finally had a place. It stopped jumping around like a kennel of puppies and turned into a wise old hound, its head resting on crossed paws.

I felt convinced that this was the ultimate form of my magic. At fifteen, I’d already discovered my final form.

But I could not move. I wanted to grab the moon, just like my vision, but my body didn’t respond to the signals of my brain. In fact, it was exhausting me. I might have discovered my final form, but I didn’t know how to control it.

And just like that, I shrunk. Really fast. I was just big enough to be in the stratosphere before I started to fall. I saw houses rise up from the ground, and people turn from little spots into living creatures. There was night and day, stars, planes.

Then there was water. I knew that if I landed in the ocean at this speed, I would die.

I could not move my body. The magic had tired me out, made my muscles numb. There was nothing I could do but fall.

A couple hundred feet before reaching the water, something happened. I could see a brown spot from the corner of my eye, which I imagined to be a ship. Before I hit the water, the water itself seemed to lift up. There was a giant hand of ocean water. It grabbed me out of the air like a catcher in a baseball match.

The water, somehow, felt soft. It kept me under for a few seconds. When I came up to breathe, the hand had turned into a wave. I guess it felt like the sensation of a flying carpet.

It was bringing me towards the ship I had seen seconds earlier.

I was barely able to keep my eyes open. I couldn’t move my body. And I was naked.

There were hands touching me, voices. Their faces were blurry. Someone put a blanket around me.

Someone slapped me in the face. I woke up.

‘What the hell were you doing?’ said Gajeel. I blinked. He was kneeling in front of me, looking angry as hell. Eric, the pirate with the blue hair, stood behind him, his arms crossed. The eye which had previously been covered by his hair was exposed. But instead of an eye there was a binocular.

‘I was just,’ I muttered, and I felt I was losing consciousness. Gajeel slapped me again.

‘Don’t fall back. What did you do?’

I leaned against the mast of the ship. The sound of the flag waving was soothing, but I had to stay awake. ‘I discovered...my final...form.’

‘What?’ That was Eric. ‘But you’re like...twelve. How the hell did you do that?’

Gajeel squinted his eyes at me, as if he didn’t believe me. But when he spoke, it turned out he did. ‘I should’ve known this would happen. There’s too much magic here.’

My consciousness was slowly seeping back in and I started questioning if this was real. Gajeel had motion sickness, why was he on this ship?

‘Kid,’ he said, and he grabbed my shoulder, ‘listen to me. What you did was stupid, but cool, okay? It’s stuff you can do here, where there is enough magic. But if you do that back home, at your school, you’re dead.’ He squeezed his fingers deeper into my shoulder. ‘Do you understand? Only here.’

I nodded. ‘I understand.’

I never told anyone about that night. Eric took me and Gajeel back to the island. I never discovered how they found me, and I never asked. I was just glad to be alive.

But I never made a promise I wouldn’t use my final form here. Gajeel just warned me what would happen if I did.

There was another explosion. Floyd lay on the ground, his head bleeding. Rose was barely breathing, Lily was nowhere to be seen. All of a sudden it was just me facing El Paco. And I knew what to do.

I didn’t even know if it would work. There wasn’t enough magic, which meant I had to pull it from myself. My body had already proved it was capable of hosting large amounts of magic. Maybe there was still some left from the visit to Lux.

I folded my fists and took deep breaths. Ever since that night, I’d been dwelling on what to call my final form. On the way back here, I’d finally landed on the perfect one.

‘Ah, you again,’ grinned El Paco. There was smoke all around him, seemingly coming from nowhere. ‘This should be easy.’

Floyd groaned and pushed himself off the ground. At least he was alive. ‘Layton, get out of here.’

I closed my eyes and by that closed myself off from my surroundings. I returned to the ocean, to the moon dancing in the water, to the silence. I started to grow.

I opened my eyes and said: ‘Atlas Mode.’
